## How to Set Up in Mailchimp

### Step 1: Create a Mailchimp Account
1. Go to mailchimp.com and sign up for free (up to 500 contacts)
2. Create a new audience for your leads

### Step 2: Connect Google Sheets to Mailchimp
1. Use Zapier (free tier) to connect your Google Sheets to Mailchimp
2. Set up a Zap: "When new row is added to Google Sheets â†’ Add subscriber to Mailchimp"
3. Map the fields: Name, Email, Phone, Business Type, Budget

### Step 3: Create Email Automation
1. In Mailchimp, go to Automations â†’ Create â†’ Custom
2. Set trigger: "When subscriber is added to audience"
3. Add 4 emails with the templates above
4. Set delays: Email 1 (immediate), Email 2 (+2 days), Email 3 (+4 days), Email 4 (+7 days)

### Step 4: Personalize
Replace these placeholders in each email:
- `[Name]` - Subscriber's first name
- `[Business Type]` - From their form submission
- `[Budget Range]` - From their form submission
- `[Your Name]` - Your actual name
- `[Calendly Link]` - Your actual Calendly booking link
- `[Date]` - Use Mailchimp's date merge tags

### Pro Tips
- Test the sequence by adding yourself as a test subscriber
- Track open rates and click rates in Mailchimp dashboard
- A/B test subject lines to improve open rates
- Personalize Email 3's bonus tip based on business type
- Update Email 4's urgency dates monthly to keep it current

## Alternative: Manual Email Sequence

If you prefer not to use Mailchimp initially, you can:
1. Check your Google Sheets daily for new submissions
2. Copy/paste these templates into Gmail
3. Manually customize and send based on the schedule
4. Set calendar reminders for follow-ups

This works fine when starting out (under 10 leads/week), but automation saves significant time as you scale.
